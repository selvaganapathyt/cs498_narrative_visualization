COVID-19 USA Stats & Trends

For the last 6 months,   the COVID-19 pandemic has affected millions of lives in the U.S. Does the current stats and trends show any sign of decreasing cases/deaths ?


Non Flat Cumulative cases

The chart shows the cumulative confirmed cases trend from late Jan 2020 till July 2020.

The ongoing COVID-19 pandemic was confirmed to have reached the United States in January 2020. Since then, the trend has been surging and showing no signs of slowing down.

Even though lockdown has been implemented across different states, it had little impact on controlling the cases as the number of coronavirus cases kept growing. More than 3 million people in the U.S. have tested positive so far...


How to interact with the chart ?

- Select State dropdown to view the cumulative cases trend for a specific state.

- Hover over the line for the tooltip to display cumulative cases count as of specific date.

Increase in New Cases

Inital wave of coronavirus cases in USA attained peak during 1st week of April.

Since then, various measures like lockdown and mandatory masks had brought down the number of cases.

Even though coronavirus cases across the United States dropped since mid June, several states are still setting distressing records. As states through out the U.S. lift stay-at-home orders, reopen businesses, and relax social distancing measures, this graph shows a increase trend in the COVID-19 cases.

How to interact with the chart ?

- Select State dropdown to view the new cases trend for a specific state.

- Hover over the line for the tooltip to display new cases count as of specific date.

Flattening Death Cases

Though Coronavirus cases are increasing, the death cases started to decrease. The first death in the US was reported on February 29, 2020.The country reached 100,000 deaths mark on May 27, 2020.

Attempts like "Working from home" and "social distancing" helped to flatten the curve.

How to interact with the chart ?

- Select State dropdown to view the cumulative death trend for a specific state.

- Hover over the line for the tooltip to display cumulative deaths count as of specific date.

Decreasing New Death Cases

USA goverment reacted very lately to control the Coronavirus cases. Because of the lack in awareness, a huge surge in death cases was seen between March and April.

Since April, various measures like lockdown and mandatory masks had brought down the number of deaths significantly

Since July, as states through out the U.S. lift stay-at-home orders, reopen businesses, and relax social distancing measures, this graph shows a small increase in the COVID-19 deaths.

How to interact with the chart ?

- Hover over the line for the tooltip to display new deaths count as of specific date.

Fatality Trend

Infection Fatality Rate, measured as the ratio between confirmed deaths and confirmed cases is expressed as a percentage and represents a measure of disease severity. High fatality rates indicates high COVID disease severity

Based on this chart, its evident that , because of the early outbreak impact in North Eastern region, States like New York, New Jersey and Connecticut has highest fatality rates when compared to other states in the US.

The fatality trend looks low/meduim for most of the Non North Eastern states which is still a good sign.

How to interact with the chart ?

- Click on the "Severity legend" highlight the countries which share the same severity

- Hover over the circles for the tooltip to display fatality trend specific details like Country, fatality rate etc.
